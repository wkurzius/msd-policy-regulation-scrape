ELANOnline District Policies Header Manville Board of Education Home To Regulation Search District Policies District Policies TOC District Policy 4217 - USE OF CORPORAL PUNISHMENT Section: Program Date Created: September 2023 Date Edited: September 2023 No support staff member employed by the Board or a person engaged in any school in the school district shall inflict or cause to be inflicted corporal punishment upon a student attending any school in the school district; but any such support staff member may, within the scope of their employment, use and apply such amounts of force as is reasonable and necessary: 1. To quell a disturbance, threatening physical injury to others; 2. To obtain possession of weapons or other dangerous objects upon the person or within the control of a student; 3. For the purpose of self-defense; and 4. For the protection of persons or property; and such acts, or any of them, shall not be construed to constitute corporal punishment pursuant to N.J.S.A. 18A:6-1. Every resolution, bylaw, rule, ordinance, or other act or authority permitting or authorizing corporal punishment to be inflicted upon a student attending any school in the district shall be void. Any support staff member employed by the Board found to have violated this Policy may be subject to discipline by the Board. N.J.S.A. 18A:6-1; 18A:37-1 Adopted: 19 September 2023
